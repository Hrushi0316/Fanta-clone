About Fanta

Fanta is a fruit-flavored carbonated soft drink created by The Coca-Cola Company. It was first introduced in Germany in 1940 as a result of trade embargoes during World War II that made it difficult to import Coca-Cola syrup. Fanta quickly gained popularity and expanded to other countries after the war. Today, Fanta is available in a variety of flavors worldwide, with orange being the most popular. The brand is known for its vibrant colors, fruity taste, and fun marketing campaigns targeting younger audiences.
